Who I Am

robotics enthusiast with passion for autonomous technologies

I am a second year robotics graduate student at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. I graduated with a bachelors degree in Mechanical Engineering from Birla Institute of Technology and Science, Pilani in 2016, after which I experienced a brief but enjoyable stint at ExxonMobil Corporation.

In the past few years I have worked on many independent projects alongside my formal education, with projects ranging from full blown formula SAE cars to tiny autonomous swarm robots. My goal is to develop innovative solutions in the industrial and domestic automation industries through my combined software and hardware experience.

Featured Works

These are some of the recent projects I've worked on.

NASA Astronet

A Human-Centric Network of Co-Robots

Project Link

The Astrobee is an autonomous free-flying robot developed by NASA to assist astronauts on the International Space Station. Under the guest scientist program, I develop multi-agent coverage and control algorithms focussed on assisting humans in space. The work is performed under the guidance of Dr. Dimitra Panagou under the NASA ECF grant.

AUVSI RobotX

Autonomous Surface Vessel Competition

Project Link

The RobotX competition by the AUVSI Foundation is the most complex robotics competition till date. Our team at Michigan is participating this December with the ultimate objective of developing a fully autonomous surface vessel aimed to further the advances in oceanic exploration.

PoseNet++

Deep Learning in SLAM

Project Link

PoseNet++ is a deep learning framework for passive SLAM capable of re-localizing a robot within 2m of it's ground truth in medium scale environments. It provides a viable solution for the kidnapped robot problem and an accurate solution to the SLAM problem in general.

Path Planning | Exploration

Branding

Project Link

As a part of my undergraduate thesis, I develop a simulator for autonomous multi-agent exploration and path planning in unknown environments. The code is initially developed for a Python Simulator, which is then used on a swarm of TurtleBots.

Kinematics Simulator

JavaScript based 3D Robot Kinematics Simulator

Project Link

A JavaScript simulator based on the Robot WebTools is developed to simulate robot kinematics and make control of robots such as the Fetch robot multi-platform by using a web interface for the Robot Operating System.

Simultaneous Localization & Mapping

Particle Filter based SLAM on a differential drive robot

Project Link

Pedestrian Tracking

Mask R-CNN based Pedestrian Tracking

Project Link

A robust pedestrian tracking algorithm is implemented in this project based on Mask R-CNN, Optical Flow and Particle Filtering. The system is capable of single hypothesis tracking in difficult lighting conditions.
